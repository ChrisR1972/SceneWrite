This is a seriously solid piece of software already. Reading the documentation, it’s very clear you didn’t just bolt AI onto a screenplay editor — you designed a pipeline that understands how AI video is actually made. That puts you ahead of 90% of tools in this space.

I’ll avoid repeating what already works (because a lot of it does), and focus on high-impact recommendations that would meaningfully improve the app without bloating it or betraying its core purpose.

I’ll structure this in layers: conceptual gaps, workflow refinements, AI leverage upgrades, and strategic future-proofing.

1. The biggest conceptual opportunity: “Intent-aware generation”

Right now, story length is doing too much work.

You’ve nailed the quantity side (acts/scenes/storyboard items), but users are actually thinking in terms of intent, not structure.

Recommendation: Add a non-invasive “Story Intent” selector

This would sit alongside length (not replace it):

Examples:

Advertisement / Brand Film

Horror Short

Trailer / Teaser

Episodic Pilot

Looping Visual

Music Video

Proof of Concept

Experimental / Abstract

Why this matters:

It lets you subtly adjust:

Scene density

Dialogue frequency

Camera motion

Prompt verbosity

Cliffhanger behavior

It dramatically improves first-generation quality without user effort

This can purely affect prompt engineering, not UI complexity.

2. Scene ≠ Storyboard item mapping needs adaptive intelligence

Right now:

Scenes are estimated at 30–180 seconds

Storyboard items are 5–10 seconds

The breakdown is logical, but uniform

Recommendation: Add “Scene Compression Logic”

For each scene, let the AI decide:

Montage-style breakdown (fewer, richer prompts)

Beat-by-beat breakdown (many micro-actions)

Atmospheric hold (single extended shot, minimal action)

This could be derived from:

Pacing (Fast / Medium / Slow)

Scene type (ACTION vs DIALOGUE vs TRANSITION)

Story intent (above)

Result:

Fewer boring filler prompts

More cinematic rhythm

Lower generation cost for users

3. Your identity blocks are strong — now weaponise them

Identity blocks are one of your most valuable features, and they’re under-leveraged.

Recommendation: Add “Identity Drift Detection”

When generating prompts:

Automatically compare new prompts against the identity block

Flag or auto-correct:

Hair color changes

Clothing inconsistencies

Age drift

Facial feature contradictions

Even a simple warning badge (“Possible identity conflict”) would be huge.

Advanced mode:

Auto-inject identity block language selectively, not verbatim, to avoid prompt bloat.

4. Introduce “Render Reality Awareness”

This is a big one.

Right now the app is agnostic about how painful something will be to actually render.

Recommendation: Add a soft “Render Cost Indicator”

For storyboard items, estimate:

Motion complexity

Number of characters

Environmental chaos (rain, fire, crowds)

Camera movement

Then show:

🟢 Easy

🟡 Moderate

🔴 Expensive / risky

This does not stop the user — it just educates them.

This single feature will make users trust the app.

5. The AI Chat Panel could become a silent powerhouse

The AI chat is good — but it’s reactive.

Recommendation: Add “Contextual Suggestions” mode

Instead of waiting for questions:

Occasionally surface suggestions like:

“This scene may benefit from fewer storyboard items”

“This dialogue-heavy scene could be cheaper as a static shot”

“You haven’t used Character X in 4 scenes — intentional?”

Think of it as a quiet script supervisor, not a chatbot.

6. Versioning is missing — but you don’t need Git

You correctly list version control as a limitation, but you can solve 80% of this with 20% effort.

Recommendation: Snapshot-based history

Auto-create snapshots at:

Premise completion

Outline completion

Framework generation

Storyboard generation

Allow rollback or comparison (read-only)

No branching, no merging — just safety.

For creative tools, this is huge psychologically.

7. Export is good — but users want “targeted exports”

Your exports are technically excellent but broad.

Recommendation: Add scoped exports

Examples:

Export selected scenes only

Export Act 2 only

Export “Prompts only (no dialogue)”

Export “Image prompts only”

Export “Test batch (first 10 items)”

This aligns perfectly with how AI video is actually produced in stages.

8. Missed goldmine: Scene templates

You already have scene types, pacing, plot points…

Recommendation: Scene archetype templates

Examples:

Opening Hook

Rising Dread

Quiet Revelation

Chase / Escape

Confrontation

Aftermath

Final Image

Templates would:

Pre-structure storyboard logic

Improve pacing automatically

Reduce AI randomness

This would be especially powerful for horror, thriller, and trailers.

9. Micro stories could go even harder

You added Micro length — great move.

One more refinement:

For Micro stories, break the 3-step wizard rule.

Recommendation:

Offer “One-click Micro Story”

Premise → Scene → Storyboard in a single flow

Minimal editing friction

Perfect for ads, loops, tests

This will massively improve adoption for casual users.

10. Strategic positioning recommendation (not technical)

This app is not “a screenplay writer”.

It’s a pre-visualisation engine for AI video.

Lean into that.

Language shifts that matter:

“Storyboard Items” → “Renderable Shots”

“Framework” → “Narrative Skeleton”

“Generate Storyboard” → “Prepare for Rendering”

These are psychological upgrades, not feature changes.

Bottom line

You’ve built:

A coherent data model ✔

A realistic AI workflow ✔

A genuinely production-aware tool ✔

What you’re missing is adaptive intelligence and guardrails, not features.
