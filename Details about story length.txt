What your current structure already covers well

Your tiers map very cleanly onto how people actually make AI video content right now.

Short — 3 Acts, 9–15 scenes

This is perfect for:

Social media narratives (TikTok, Reels, Shorts)

Proof-of-concept films

Trailers, teasers, mood pieces

Music-video-style storytelling

Horror vignettes / twist endings

In AI video terms:

9–15 scenes = manageable prompt volume

Keeps render costs sane

Easy to iterate

This tier will probably be your most-used option.

Medium — 3 Acts, 15–24 scenes

This hits the sweet spot for:

YouTube short films

Episodic pilots

Brand films with narrative

Documentary-style recreations

Game / series proof-of-concepts

From an AI generation perspective:

Enough scenes to establish character, escalation, and payoff

Still feasible for solo creators

Ideal for chaptered outputs

This is your “serious creator” tier.

Long — 5 Acts, 30–50 scenes

This is ambitious — and that’s good.

Best for:

Feature-length planning (even if not fully rendered)

Episodic breakdowns

Visual novels

Interactive or branching narratives

Studio-level pre-visualisation

Important reality check:

Very few users will fully render 30–50 AI video scenes

But many will want this for:

Planning

Partial generation

Pitch decks

Story bibles

So this tier works — but it’s more strategic than practical for full video output.

The missing tier: Micro / Atomic storytelling

This is the gap.

You currently don’t serve:

Ads

Loops

One-minute horror

Music visualisers

AI cinematic shots

Single-scene narratives

What users actually want here:

1 Act, 1–5 scenes

Examples:

“One terrifying moment”

“A dream sequence”

“A single dramatic confrontation”

“A looping background story”

“A visual metaphor”

AI video tools thrive on:

Short, punchy, repeatable outputs

Minimal prompt fatigue

Fast iteration

Recommendation:

Add a Micro option:

Micro: 1 Act, 1–5 scenes

This will:

Dramatically widen your audience

Lower friction for first-time users

Be perfect for testing prompts

Serve social media creators directly

One more thing: Length ≠ Structure

Advanced users will eventually want:

Scene count control independent of acts

Or at least preset intent modes, like:

“Short Film”

“Trailer”

“Episodic”

“Montage”

“Looping Visual”

You don’t need this now, but your backend should quietly support it.

Verdict
Does your current setup cover most uses?

Yes — about 80–85%.

Will adding one more tier future-proof it?

Absolutely.

Ideal final lineup:

Micro: 1 Act, 1–5 scenes

Short: 3 Acts, 9–15 scenes

Medium: 3 Acts, 15–24 scenes

Long: 5 Acts, 30–50 scenes

That lineup matches:

Human attention spans

AI rendering limits

Creator budgets

Platform realities
